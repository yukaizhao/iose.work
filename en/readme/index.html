        Intent-Oriented Software Engineering: A Paradigm Revolution from Code to Intent

        Introduction

        This is a comprehensive guide to the future of software engineering. As large-scale AI agents gain powerful execution capabilities, humanity’s core value will shift upward from “How to implement” to “What to achieve” and “Why it matters.”

        This book originates from an in-depth dialogue spanning dozens of sessions, systematically exploring the complete journey from traditional software engineering to Intent-Oriented Software Engineering (IOSE). Whether you’re a technical leader, architect, developer, or entrepreneur, you’ll gain profound insights and practical guidance about the future of software engineering.

        Core Concepts Overview

        ConceptDefinition
        IntentClear expression of “what to achieve” and “why,” rather than “how to do it”
        Business IntentTop-level business goals describing value propositions and business logic
        Technical IntentLow-level technical constraints guiding agents in implementing business intent
        Constraint NetworkCollection of multi-dimensional constraints including architecture, security, performance, and complexity
        Intent EngineerNew role responsible for transforming business requirements into structured intent
        Agent EngineerTechnical role responsible for maintaining constraint networks and agent clusters
        Intent LibraryKnowledge asset repository containing high-quality intent templates and constraint rules
